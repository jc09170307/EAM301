🐲 Core Question Frameworks

🏯 "Why did this medical idea emerge at THIS historical moment? What conditions made it possible or necessary?"
→ The Context Question — most common Socratic frame in EAM301
The core analytical move: for any development, ask — What dynasty? What political stability? Who had access to medical knowledge? What was the relationship between folk medicine and court medicine? External influences (trade routes, invasions, contact)? Historical medicine doesn't happen in a vacuum. Dynasty = context = exam answer. Zhang Zhong Jing wrote Shang Han Lun because epidemics killed 2/3 of his family. Heo Jun wrote Dongui Bogam because the Imjin War destroyed Korean medicine. Context drives innovation.
⚖️ "What did THIS figure/text CHANGE versus what came before? What problem were they solving?"
→ The Developmental Question — compare, contrast, trace evolution
Show you understand DEVELOPMENT — medicine as a living tradition responding to clinical realities. Zhang Zhong Jing: theory → clinical protocols (epidemic urgency). Ye Tian Shi: Shang Han Lun couldn't treat warm/febrile diseases → Four Levels. Heo Jun: Chinese herbs unaffordable after Imjin War → Korean herbs. The PROBLEM always explains the INNOVATION.
🇰🇷 "What is the significance for the KOREAN tradition? How did Korean medicine receive, adapt, or develop Chinese medicine independently?"
→ Korean Lineage Question — Choi's specialty, expect exam depth here
Prof. Choi (KMD) specializes in Korean medicine. EXAM PRIORITY: Dongui Bogam (1613) — commissioned after Imjin War, used Korean herbs (not Chinese imports), democratized medicine, 25 volumes, UNESCO heritage. Sasang Constitutional Medicine (1894) — Lee Je-ma, UNIQUELY Korean, NOT derived from Chinese sources, same disease = different herb by constitutional type. Four types: Taeyang (<1%), Soyang (~30%), Taeeum (~50%), Soeum (~20%).
📜 "What does 'Naked History' mean? Why does stripping away mythology matter for understanding Eastern medicine?"
→ Meta-Question — likely Day 1 AND both exams
"Naked History" = stripping away mythology, hagiography, official narrative to find what actually happened. The Nei Jing attributed to Yellow Emperor, Shen Nong Ben Cao to Shen Nong — NOT written by those people. Court scholars used legendary attribution to give texts imperial authority. Critical reading: who wrote these texts, who had economic incentives, what was suppressed. This is Choi's core methodology — his father was a history teacher, this is personal.
🔮 "What is Bi Lei Qu Xiang and why is analogical reasoning the epistemological foundation of Asian herbal medicine?"
→ Reasoning Method Question — explains HOW classical medicine thought
比類取象 Bǐ Lèi Qǔ Xiàng = "comparing categories, drawing from images" = analogical reasoning. Instead of Western mechanistic reasoning, AHM reasons by analogy: a root growing downward might descend rebellious Qi; red herbs treat Blood; bitter taste drains Heat. Not superstition — a different epistemological framework that worked clinically for 2,000+ years. Explains the entire structure of Chinese materia medica.
🔍 "What is the Mawangdui evidence and why does it challenge the traditional narrative of acupuncture's origins?"
→ Archaeological Evidence Question — empirical history of acupuncture
Mawangdui texts (馬王堆, discovered 1973, dated ~168 BCE) = oldest medical manuscripts in China. Describe ~11 channels with NO acupoints — proves meridian theory developed GRADUALLY, not revealed whole. Classical 12-channel + 365-acupoint system appeared later in Nei Jing. Supports "Naked History": acupuncture was built up over centuries. The system we use today is a historical construction, not a timeless revelation.
🏛️ "Why did every East Asian country show simultaneous abolition AND revival of traditional medicine in the 20th century?"
→ Modernization Question — medicine as politics and identity
Pattern: Western-educated modernizers tried abolition ("unscientific") → popular resistance + nationalist identity forced revival. China: 1929 abolition attempt reversed; 1950 Mao mandated integration; 1959 TCM standardized (Porkert, Kaptchuk, Maciocia). Korea: Japanese colonial suppression → post-independence revival. Japan: Meiji suppression → Kampo revival, Koho-ha vs. Kokan Igaku split. Medicine is not just science — it's politics, economics, and cultural identity.

🐉 歷史 · History Timeline

Figures + dates + WHY = high-yield exam content
Eastern Medicine — 4,700 Years of Development

🏯 Chinese Medicine · Major Figures

c. 2700 BCE · Legendary
🐲 Yellow Emperor (黃帝) & Shen Nong (神農)
Naked History point: NOT real authors. Court scholars used imperial names to give texts unquestionable authority. Nei Jing = Yellow Emperor + Qi Bo dialogue. Shen Nong Ben Cao = Divine Farmer. WHY? Political legitimation. Authority. Imperial endorsement. This is the core of Choi's "Naked History" approach.
c. 168 BCE · Han Dynasty — KEY EXAM POINT
🔍 Mawangdui Medical Texts (馬王堆)
Oldest medical manuscripts (discovered 1973). Describe ~11 channels with NO acupoints — proves meridian theory developed GRADUALLY. The complete 12-channel + 365-acupoint system came later. Supports "Naked History": classical acupuncture was built over centuries, not revealed whole.
c. 100 BCE · Han Dynasty
📚 Huang Di Nei Jing (黃帝內經)
Su Wen (素問) — theory: Yin/Yang, Five Phases, organ systems. Ling Shu (靈樞) — acupuncture channels, needling. Compiled by Han court scholars. Foundation of all subsequent Eastern medicine theory. Han Dynasty unification → systematization of knowledge across the empire.
c. 100 BCE · Han Dynasty
🌿 Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing (神農本草經)
365 medicinals in 3 grades: Superior (君 non-toxic, life-prolonging), Middle (臣 therapeutic), Inferior (佐 toxic, specific conditions). Foundation of herbal classification. NOT written by Shen Nong — compiled during Han to systematize accumulated folk knowledge.
c. 150–219 CE · Han/Three Kingdoms
🏥 Zhang Zhong Jing (張仲景) — "Sage of Medicine"
Shang Han Lun (傷寒論) — Six Stages framework. Context = WHY: devastating epidemics (possibly typhoid) killed 2/3 of his family in 10 years. First systematic CLINICAL manual — moved from cosmological theory to treatment protocols. The bridge from theory to bedside practice.
AD 281–341 · Jin Dynasty
☯️ Ge Hong (葛洪) — Daoist Physician
Zhou Hou Bei Ji Fang (肘後備急方) — emergency formulas for common people. Referenced 青蒿 qinghao (sweet wormwood) for fever → Tu Youyou isolated artemisinin and won the 2015 Nobel Prize. 1,700-year gap between classical reference and modern validation.
AD 581–682 · Tang Dynasty — "Golden Age"
👑 Sun Si Miao (孫思邈) — "King of Medicine" 藥王
Bei Ji Qian Jin Yao Fang (備急千金要方). First Chinese medical ethics text — great physician treats all equally. Gynecology and pediatrics sections (previously neglected). Daoist longevity integrated with clinical medicine. Lived ~100 years. Tang = cosmopolitan Silk Road empire → state medical institutions.
AD 1115–1234 · Jin/Yuan Dynasties
⚖️ The Four Masters of Jin-Yuan (金元四大家)
North China under Jurchen rule → different climate, different diseases → four distinct clinical schools. Liu Wansu — Fire/Heat theory. Zhang Congzheng — expel pathogens. Li Dongyuan — Spleen-Stomach is root. Zhu Danxi — Yin deficiency is root. Medicine is not monolithic — clinical context shapes theory.
AD 1518–1593 · Ming Dynasty
🌿 Li Shi Zhen (李時珍) — Ben Cao Gang Mu (本草綱目)
27 years, 1,892 medicinals, 11,096 formulas, 1,110 illustrations. Greatest naturalist-physician in Chinese history. Corrected previous errors through direct observation. Translated into Latin, French, Russian — influenced global botany. Note: 脾 Pi possibly mistranslated as "pancreas" by Western scholars — functional organ concept, not purely anatomical.
AD 1798 · Qing Dynasty
🔬 Wang Qingren (王清任) — Anatomy & Blood Stasis
42 years of corpse dissection to correct classical anatomical errors. Yi Lin Gai Cuo (醫林改錯). Sophisticated Blood Stasis theory — 瘀血 yū xuè. Parallel to Greek "thrombos" — Eastern and Western medicine independently arrived at similar clinical observations through different theoretical frameworks.
AD 1667–1746 · Qing Dynasty
🔥 Ye Tian Shi (葉天士) — Warm Disease School
Wen Bing (溫病) — Four Levels: Wei (衛), Qi (氣), Ying (營), Xue (血). First to truly understand infectious nature of epidemic diseases. Liu Qi/Liu Yin (Six Excesses) analogous to modern host-pathogen theory. Studied under 17 different teachers.
1929 → 1950 → 1959 · Republic → PRC
🏛️ Modernization — Abolition & Revival
1929: Nationalist government tried abolition → popular resistance reversed it. 1950: Mao mandated integration. 1959: Full TCM standardization — Western scholars: Manfred Porkert (Germany), Ted Kaptchuk (USA), Giovanni Maciocia (Italy). Descartes/Flexner biomedical model critique: mind-body dualism as Western blind spot.

🇰🇷 Korean Medicine · Prof. Choi's Specialty (EXAM PRIORITY)

AD 1613 · Joseon Dynasty · Post-Imjin War
📚 Heo Jun (허준) — Dongui Bogam (동의보감)
Context = WHY: Japanese invasions (Imjin War 1592–98) devastated Korean medical infrastructure — physicians killed, texts destroyed, supply chains collapsed. King Seonjo commissioned Heo Jun. Key innovation: used KOREAN herbs instead of imported Chinese → democratized medicine for poor. 25 volumes. UNESCO Memory of the World 2009. "The Zhang Zhong Jing of Korea."
AD 1894 · Late Joseon
⚖️ Lee Je-ma (이제마) — Sasang Constitutional Medicine (사상의학)
Dongui Susebowon (동의수세보원). UNIQUELY Korean — NOT derived from Chinese sources. Four constitutional types: Taeyang (太陽, very rare <1%), Taeeum (太陰, ~50%), Soyang (少陽, ~30%), Soeum (少陰, ~20%). Same disease = different herb by constitutional type. Revolutionary individualization — predating personalized medicine by 130 years.
20th–21st Century
🏫 Korean Oriental Medicine — Modernization
Japanese colonial suppression → post-independence revival. 1965: Kyung Hee University — first OM university in Korea. Today: KMD license separate from MD. Korean medicine legally and clinically distinct from Chinese medicine within Korea. Prof. Choi (KMD) represents this distinct tradition.

🌪️ Liu Qi · Six Pathogenic Factors

🔬
Host-Pathogen Theory Connection Liu Qi/Liu Yin = Wind, Cold, Summer-Heat, Dampness, Dryness, Fire. Disease occurs when host Wei Qi is insufficient. Analogous to modern host-pathogen theory. Max von Pettenkofer's 1892 cholera self-experiment: drank cholera culture, didn't get sick — proving HOST susceptibility matters. Eastern medicine made this same observation centuries earlier.

🌿 Landmark Formulas

Gui Zhi Tang
桂枝湯
Shang Han Lun's foundational formula. Tai Yang exterior deficiency (with sweating). Cinnamon + white peony + ginger + jujube + licorice. Building block of classical formulation.
Ma Huang Tang
麻黃湯
Tai Yang exterior excess (no sweating). Ma huang + gui zhi + almond + licorice. Archetypal cold-expelling formula of the Shang Han tradition.
Si Jun Zi Tang
四君子湯
Four Gentlemen — foundation of Spleen Qi tonification. Ren shen + bai zhu + fu ling + zhi gan cao. Base of Li Dongyuan's Spleen-Stomach school.
Yin Qiao San
銀翹散
Warm Disease — Wei level Heat. Honeysuckle + forsythia + mint. Cool-pungent (NOT warm-pungent like Shang Han). Key differentiation.
