🐲 Core Question Frameworks

🏯 "Why did this medical idea emerge at THIS historical moment? What social, political, or cultural conditions made it possible?"
→ The Context Question — most common Socratic frame. Dynasty = context = exam answer.
Core analytical move: for any development, ask — What dynasty? What political stability? Who had access to medicine? What was court vs. folk medicine? External influences? Zhang Zhong Jing: epidemic killed 2/3 of family → Shang Han Lun. Heo Jun: Imjin War → Dongui Bogam. Ye Tian Shi: Wen Bing inadequacy of Shang Han Lun → Four Levels. Context DRIVES innovation — always connect WHY.
📜 "What does 'Naked History' mean? Why does stripping mythology matter for understanding Eastern medicine's development?"
→ Meta-Question — certain on both exams. Core to Choi's entire approach.
"Naked History" = stripping mythology, hagiography, official narrative to find what actually happened. Nei Jing attributed to Yellow Emperor, Ben Cao to Shen Nong — NOT real authors. Court scholars used legendary attribution to give texts imperial authority. Critical reading: who wrote these texts, who funded them, what was suppressed. Author's father was a history teacher — this is personal to Choi. Every "legendary" attribution is a political act.
🇰🇷 "What makes Korean medicine an independent tradition, not just a copy of Chinese medicine?"
→ Korean Lineage Question — Choi's specialty, expect maximum exam depth here
Dongui Bogam (1613): commissioned after Imjin War devastated Korea, used KOREAN herbs not Chinese imports → democratized medicine. Sasang Constitutional Medicine (1894): Lee Je-ma, NOT derived from Chinese sources, same disease = different herb by constitutional type. 4 types: Taeyang (<1%), Soyang (~30%), Taeeum (~50%), Soeum (~20%). Heo Im's dry-needling innovation. Kim Deokbang's acupuncture techniques. KMD license separate from MD. This is INDEPENDENT development, not imitation.
🔍 "What does the Mawangdui evidence reveal about the traditional narrative of acupuncture's origins?"
→ Archaeological Evidence — supports Naked History, proves gradual development
Mawangdui texts (168 BCE, discovered 1973) = oldest medical manuscripts in China. Describe ~11 channels with NO acupoints — proves meridian theory developed GRADUALLY, not revealed whole. Classical 12-channel + 365-acupoint system appeared later in Nei Jing. This supports "Naked History": acupuncture was built over centuries by practitioners, not revealed to the Yellow Emperor in a moment of divine inspiration. Archaeological evidence > textual mythology.
☯️ "How did Taoist philosophy shape the theoretical foundation of Eastern medicine?"
→ Philosophical Foundation — connects Mythos to Logos, explains the WHY of theory
Taoism gave EAM: (1) naturalistic observation — observe patterns in nature, not supernatural intervention; (2) non-interference — support body's own healing; (3) Yin-Yang as dynamic balance — not good vs. evil, but complementary forces; (4) Five Phases from cosmological observation; (5) Bi Lei Qu Xiang (比類取象) — analogical reasoning: what resembles must be related. The Warring States period (intellectual explosion) allowed Taoist ideas to be systematized into medical theory. Without Taoism, there is no Nei Jing.
🌍 "Why did every East Asian country show simultaneous abolition AND revival of traditional medicine in the 20th century?"
→ Modernization Question — connects medicine, politics, identity, and science
Pattern: Western-educated modernizers tried abolition ("unscientific") → popular demand + nationalist identity forced revival. China: 1929 abolition reversed; 1950 Mao mandated integration; 1959 TCM standardized (Porkert, Kaptchuk, Maciocia). Korea: Japanese colonial suppression → post-independence revival, 1965 Kyung Hee University. Japan: Meiji suppression → Kampo revival, Koho-ha vs. Kokan Igaku split. Taiwan: preserved classical tradition. Medicine is not just science — it is politics, economics, and cultural identity. "Worship or Ignore?" = false binary.
⚗️ "How did the artemisinin discovery validate 1,700 years of classical Chinese medical knowledge?"
→ East-West Convergence — shows classical texts contain real empirical knowledge
Ge Hong (281–341 CE) wrote in Zhou Hou Bei Ji Fang: "Take a handful of qinghao (青蒿/sweet wormwood), soak in water, squeeze out the juice, and drink it" for fever/malaria. 1,700 years later, Tu Youyou read this classical text, extracted artemisinin using Ge Hong's cold-extraction method (not heat — preserves the compound), and won the 2015 Nobel Prize in Medicine. This proves: (1) classical texts contain real empirical observations; (2) "Naked History" reveals practical knowledge hidden under mythology; (3) EAM observation preceded Western pharmacology by millennia.

🐉 歷史 · History Timeline

Figures + dates + WHY = high-yield exam content · Know each: WHO · WHEN · WHAT · WHY
4,700 Years of Eastern Medicine Development

🏯 Chinese Medicine · Major Figures (Exam 1 Priority)

c. 2700 BCE · Legendary
🐲 Three Sovereigns — Fu Xi, Shen Nong, Yellow Emperor
Naked History point: NOT real historical figures. Court scholars used legendary attribution to give texts imperial authority. Fu Xi — bagua/trigrams, cosmological foundation. Shen Nong — divine farmer, tasted 365 plants, attributed to Ben Cao. Yellow Emperor — dialogue with Qi Bo forms literary framework of Nei Jing. Political legitimation through mythological authorship.
c. 500 BCE · Spring & Autumn Period
🩺 Bian Que (扁鵲) — First Historical Physician
Legendary/semi-historical physician credited with systematizing the four diagnostic methods: observation (望 wàng), listening/smelling (聞 wén), questioning (問 wèn), pulse-taking (切 qiè). Known as the "Father of Diagnosis." Said to have died of jealousy — a court physician had him assassinated for being too skilled.
c. 168 BCE · Han Dynasty — KEY EXAM POINT
🔍 Mawangdui Medical Texts (馬王堆)
Oldest medical manuscripts in China (discovered 1973, tomb dated 168 BCE). Describe ~11 channels with NO acupoints — proves meridian theory developed GRADUALLY. The complete 12-channel + 365-acupoint system appeared later. Supports "Naked History": acupuncture was built over centuries by practitioners, not divinely revealed.
c. 100 BCE · Han Dynasty
📚 Huang Di Nei Jing (黃帝內經) — The Foundation
Su Wen (素問 Basic Questions) — theory: Yin/Yang, Five Phases, organ systems, causes of disease, diagnostic principles. Ling Shu (靈樞 Spiritual Pivot) — acupuncture channels, needle techniques, point locations. Compiled by Han court scholars, NOT by Yellow Emperor. Han Dynasty unification → imperial need to systematize all knowledge under one authority.
c. 100 BCE · Han Dynasty
🌿 Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing (神農本草經)
365 medicinals in 3 grades: Superior (君 Jun — non-toxic, life-prolonging, taken long-term), Middle (臣 Chen — therapeutic, some toxicity), Inferior (佐 Zuo — toxic, treat specific conditions, short-term). Foundation of herbal classification. NOT written by Shen Nong — compiled during Han to systematize accumulated folk knowledge under mythological authority.
c. 150–219 CE · Han/Three Kingdoms
🏥 Zhang Zhong Jing (張仲景) — "Sage of Medicine" 醫聖
Shang Han Lun (傷寒論) — Six Stages framework. Context = WHY: devastating epidemic (possibly typhoid) killed 2/3 of his family clan of 200+ people within 10 years. Grief drove systematic clinical documentation. First clinical manual — moved from cosmological theory to treatment protocols. Jinguei Yaolue (金匱要略) — internal medicine conditions. The bridge from theory to bedside practice.
AD 140–208 CE · Eastern Han
🔪 Hua Tuo (華佗) — Surgical Pioneer
First Chinese surgeon. Invented mafeisan (麻沸散) — anesthetic compound (cannabis + wine + other herbs) for abdominal surgery. Famous for treating warlord Cao Cao's headaches. Executed 208 CE by Cao Cao (refused to stay as personal physician, Cao Cao suspected treasonous intent). Burned his surgical manuscripts before death — incalculable loss to Chinese surgical tradition.
AD 215–282 · Jin Dynasty
💓 Wang Shu He (王叔和) — Pulse Classic
Mai Jing (脈經) — first systematic pulse text, 24 pulse qualities systematized. Critical contribution: EDITED AND PRESERVED Zhang Zhong Jing's Shang Han Lun — without Wang Shu He this foundational text might have been lost entirely. Textual transmission as medical contribution. Pulse diagnosis: appears globally — ancient Egyptian, Greek, Ayurvedic (Nadi) — convergent clinical observation.
AD 281–341 · Jin Dynasty
☯️ Ge Hong (葛洪) — Daoist Physician & 2015 Nobel Precursor
Zhou Hou Bei Ji Fang (肘後備急方 — Emergency Formulas to Keep Up One's Sleeve) — practical first aid for common people, accessible to non-scholars. Referenced 青蒿 qinghao (sweet wormwood) for fever: "Take a handful, soak in water, squeeze juice, drink it." Cold extraction — key detail. 1,700 years later, Tu Youyou read this, used cold extraction to isolate artemisinin, won 2015 Nobel Prize in Medicine.
AD 581–682 · Tang Dynasty — "Golden Age"
👑 Sun Si Miao (孫思邈) — "King of Medicine" 藥王
Bei Ji Qian Jin Yao Fang (備急千金要方 — Prescriptions Worth 1,000 Gold). First Chinese medical ethics text — great physician must treat all patients equally regardless of social status, wealth, appearance, ethnicity. Sections on gynecology and pediatrics (previously neglected by Chinese medicine). Daoist longevity practices integrated. Lived ~100 years. Tang = cosmopolitan Silk Road empire → state medical institutions, accessible medicine.
AD 1115–1234 · Jin/Yuan — Four Grand Masters
⚖️ 金元四大家 Jin-Yuan Four Grand Masters
North China under Jurchen Jin rule → different climate, different disease patterns → new clinical schools. Liu Wansu 劉完素 — Fire/Heat theory, cooling herbs for most conditions. Zhang Congzheng 張從正 — expel pathogens first (sweat/vomit/purge), tonification secondary. Li Dongyuan 李東垣 — Spleen-Stomach is root (Earth school), generated by wars/famine damaging digestive function. Zhu Danxi 朱丹溪 — Yin deficiency is universal root, "Yang is always excess, Yin always deficient."
AD 1518–1593 · Ming Dynasty
🌿 Li Shi Zhen (李時珍) — Ben Cao Gang Mu (本草綱目)
27 years to compile, 1,892 medicinals, 11,096 formulas, 1,110 illustrations. Greatest naturalist-physician in Chinese history. Corrected errors in previous materia medicas through DIRECT OBSERVATION — rejected unverified tradition. Translated into Latin, French, Russian — influenced global botany and natural history. Note: 脾 Pi possibly mistranslated as "pancreas" by Western scholars — functional organ concept, not purely anatomical.
AD 1667–1746 · Qing Dynasty
🔥 Ye Tian Shi (葉天士) — Warm Disease School
Wen Bing (溫病) — Four Levels theory: Wei (衛 Defensive surface), Qi (氣 Functional), Ying (營 Nutritive), Xue (血 Blood). First physician to truly understand infectious nature of epidemic diseases. KEY contrast: Shang Han Lun uses warm/acrid herbs (expel cold); Wen Bing uses cool/bitter herbs (clear heat). Studied under 17 different teachers. Context: epidemic diseases in southern China didn't respond to Shang Han protocols — problem drove innovation.
AD 1768 · Qing Dynasty
🔬 Wang Qingren (王清任) — Anatomy & Blood Stasis
42 years of corpse observation and dissection to correct classical anatomical errors in the Nei Jing. Yi Lin Gai Cuo (醫林改錯 — Correcting Errors in the Forest of Medicine). Developed sophisticated Blood Stasis theory — 瘀血 yū xuè. Parallel concept to Greek "thrombos" — Eastern and Western medicine independently arrived at similar clinical observations (clotted/stagnant blood causes disease) through completely different theoretical frameworks.
1929 → 1950 → 1959 · Republic/PRC
🏛️ TCM Modernization — Abolition, Revival, Standardization
1929: Nationalist government tried abolition → popular resistance reversed it. 1950: Mao mandated integration into national healthcare (political + practical). 1959: Full TCM standardization — key Western scholars: Manfred Porkert (Germany), Ted Kaptchuk (USA), Giovanni Maciocia (Italy). Descartes/Flexner biomedical model critique: mind-body dualism as Western medicine's conceptual blind spot. "Worship or Ignore?" = false binary Choi rejects.

🇰🇷 Korean Medicine · Prof. Choi's Specialty (EXAM PRIORITY)

AD 1613 · Joseon Dynasty · Post-Imjin War
📚 Heo Jun (허준) — Dongui Bogam (동의보감)
Context = WHY: Japanese invasions (Imjin War, 1592–98) devastated Korean medical infrastructure — physicians killed, texts destroyed, supply chains for Chinese medicinals collapsed. King Seonjo commissioned Heo Jun. Key innovation: used KOREAN herbs instead of imported Chinese → democratized medicine for poor who could not afford expensive imports. 25 volumes. UNESCO Memory of the World 2009. Still clinically referenced. "The Zhang Zhong Jing of Korea."
AD 1613 · Joseon Dynasty
🥋 Heo Im (許任) — Dry Needling Pioneer
Joseon Dynasty acupuncturist who innovated dry needling techniques. Wrote Chimgubogam (침구보감 — Mirror of Acupuncture and Moxibustion), systematizing Korean acupuncture practice. Represents Korean independent innovation in acupuncture technique — not just receiving Chinese tradition but developing it further within Korean clinical context. Heo Im + Heo Jun = two "Heos" of Joseon medicine.
AD 1894 · Late Joseon
⚖️ Lee Je-ma (이제마) — Sasang Constitutional Medicine (사상의학)
Dongui Susebowon (동의수세보원). UNIQUELY Korean — NOT derived from Chinese sources. Four constitutional types based on relative organ strength: Taeyang (太陽, very rare <1%, strong Lung weak Liver), Taeeum (太陰, ~50% most common, strong Liver weak Lung), Soyang (少陽, ~30%, strong Spleen weak Kidney), Soeum (少陰, ~20%, strong Kidney weak Spleen). Same disease = different herb by type. Revolutionary individualization — predating personalized medicine by 130 years.
20th–21st Century
🏫 Korean Oriental Medicine — Modern Independence
Japanese colonial suppression (1910–1945) → post-WWII revival. 1965: Kyung Hee University — first OM university in Korea. Today: KMD (Korean Medical Doctor) license entirely separate from MD. Korean medicine legally and clinically distinct from Chinese medicine within Korea. Government integrates KOM into national healthcare system. 📚 Chapter 2 · Philosophical Background — Yin-Yang, Five Phases, Qi
Weeks 4–5 · CO3 · Han Dynasty to Song · Yin-Yang · Five Phases · Nei Jing · Shang Han Lun
☯️
Yin-Yang: dynamic, constantly transforming — each contains the seed of the other · 太極 Taiji

📚 Nei Jing Deep Dive

  • When: Compiled c.100 BCE, Han Dynasty — NOT by Yellow Emperor
  • Su Wen (素問 Basic Questions): Theory — Yin/Yang cosmology, Five Phases, organ physiology, causes of disease, diagnostic principles, treatment philosophy
  • Ling Shu (靈樞 Spiritual Pivot): Practice — acupuncture channels, needle techniques, point locations, 365 acupoints
  • Why attributed to Yellow Emperor? Imperial authority. If the Yellow Emperor said it, no one could question it. Classic political move of the Han Dynasty to unify knowledge under the imperial umbrella.
  • Mawangdui contrast: 168 BCE tomb texts show ~11 channels, NO acupoints — proves Nei Jing's complete system came later

🔥 Five Phases Key Correspondences

  • 🌿 Wood: Liver/GB · Spring · Sour · Anger · Green · East · Eyes
  • 🔥 Fire: Heart/SI · Summer · Bitter · Joy · Red · South · Tongue
  • 🌍 Earth: Spleen/ST · Late Summer · Sweet · Worry · Yellow · Center
  • ⚙️ Metal: Lung/LI · Autumn · Pungent · Grief · White · West · Nose
  • 💧 Water: Kidney/UB · Winter · Salty · Fear · Black · North · Ears
  • 📖 Textbook analogy: Five organs = five company departments (CEO, R&D, Operations, HR, Finance)

🏥 Shang Han Lun — Zhang Zhong Jing's Revolution

  • Context = WHY: A devastating epidemic (likely typhoid/plague) killed 2/3 of his family clan of 200+ in 10 years
  • Personal grief drove systematic documentation — this is the "Naked History" of the Shang Han Lun
  • Six Stages: Tai Yang → Yang Ming → Shao Yang → Tai Yin → Shao Yin → Jue Yin
  • First systematic CLINICAL manual — moved from cosmological theory to bedside treatment protocols
  • Zhang Zhong Jing = "Sage of Medicine" (醫聖) — the bridge from philosophy to clinical practice

🔪 Hua Tuo — The Lost Surgeon

  • Only Chinese surgeon of antiquity to perform abdominal surgery
  • Mafeisan (麻沸散): anesthetic compound — cannabis + wine + herbs, given before surgery
  • Treated warlord Cao Cao's headaches. Refused to stay as personal physician.
  • Executed 208 CE by Cao Cao — burned all surgical manuscripts before death
  • Chinese surgical tradition set back centuries. The "what might have been" tragedy of EAM history.

🌍 Chapter 4 · Now and Future — Qing Dynasty, West Meets East, Modernization
Weeks 8–9 · CO3-CO4 · Wen Bing · Li Shi Zhen · Wang Qingren · Artemisinin · Kampo · TCM
🔮
Three places where Eastern and Western medicine independently arrived at the same clinical insight through different frameworks

🔥 Wen Bing vs. Shang Han — The Great Clinical Divide

  • Problem: Southern China's epidemic febrile diseases didn't respond to Shang Han Lun's warm/acrid protocols
  • Ye Tian Shi's solution: Four Levels theory — Wei (衛), Qi (氣), Ying (營), Xue (血)
  • Cool/bitter herbs for heat (not warm/acrid for cold). Opposite treatment direction.
  • Ye Tian Shi studied under 17 different teachers — his breadth made the synthesis possible
  • Exam question: "What problem was Wen Bing solving that Shang Han Lun couldn't?"

🌿 Li Shi Zhen + Ben Cao Gang Mu

  • 27 years, 1,892 medicinals, 11,096 formulas, 1,110 illustrations
  • Corrected errors in previous materia medicas through DIRECT OBSERVATION — empirical, not traditional
  • Translated into Latin, French, Russian, Japanese — influenced global botany and natural history
  • Pi (脾) possibly mistranslated as "pancreas" by Western scholars — functional organ concept vs. anatomical
  • Wang Qingren: 42 years corpse observation → Blood Stasis theory. Anatomical correction + clinical innovation.

🇯🇵 Kampo Medicine — Japan's Path

  • Japan received Chinese medicine, systematized it as Kampo (漢方)
  • Meiji era (1868+): Western medicine mandated → Kampo suppressed
  • Split: Koho-ha (古方派 Classical school) vs. Kokan Igaku (折衷 Integrated school)
  • Modern revival: Kampo now covered by National Health Insurance in Japan
  • MD's prescribe Kampo — different integration model from Korea or China

🏛️ 1959 — TCM Standardization

  • 1929: Nationalist government tried abolition → reversed by popular resistance
  • 1950: Mao mandated integration — political + practical (not enough Western doctors)
  • 1959: Full TCM standardization. Key Western scholars: Manfred Porkert (Germany), Ted Kaptchuk (USA), Giovanni Maciocia (Italy)
  • TCM = political project as much as medical standardization
  • "Worship or Ignore?" = false binary. Choi's position: critical engagement.

🔬 Acupuncture Research — Current Evidence

  • WHO recognition: acupuncture effective for 28 conditions (evidence-based) + 63 conditions (empirical)
  • Neuroimaging: acupuncture activates specific brain regions corresponding to classical point functions
  • RCT challenge: blinding is difficult — sham acupuncture itself has effects

⚖️ "Worship or Ignore?" — Choi's Answer

  • Both extremes are wrong: blind worship ignores legitimate critique; total dismissal ignores 2,500 years of clinical data
  • Critical engagement: apply modern research methods to classical practices
  • EBM (Evidence-Based Medicine) has its own blind spots: Flexner Report (1910) excluded non-Western medicine from US medical education
  • Descartes/Flexner critique: mind-body dualism as Western medicine's structural limitation
  • Integrated medicine = the future. You are training to be an integrative practitioner.
